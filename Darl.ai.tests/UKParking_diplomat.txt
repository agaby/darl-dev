On 1/01/2011 at 6:55 my vehicle, registration number RTA 151G received a ticket from your authority.

Thank you for taking into account my appeal. I am appealing on the basis that when I received a ticket on 1/01/2011 at 6:55, the vehicle in question possessed immunity under the Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations 1961. 

As Article 31 (section 1) of the Vienna Convention states, "A diplomatic agent shall enjoy immunity from the criminal jurisdiction of the receiving State. He shall also enjoy immunity from its civil and administrative jurisdiction," and Article 23 (section 1) states, "the sending State and the head of the mission shall be exempt from all national, regional or municipal dues and taxes in respect of the premises of the mission," the ticket should be cancelled not only because it forms part of the "civil and administrative jurisdiction" which my mission is exempt from, but also because the charge can be considered a municipal due or tax. 

Furthermore, as Article 22 (section 3) states, "the premises of the mission, their furnishings and other property thereon and the means of transport of the mission shall be immune from search, requisition, attachment or execution," it is impossible to proceed with enforcing any penalty notice, making the ticket both de facto and de jure unenforceable. 

For these reasons, we therefore believe that the PCN in question should be cancelled.
