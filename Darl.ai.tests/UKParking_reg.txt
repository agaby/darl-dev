

To whom it may concern, 

On 1/01/2011 at 6:55 my vehicle, registration number RTA 151G received a ticket from your authority.

Thank you for taking into account my appeal. I am appealing on the basis that I mis-stated my registration as 'CMX 981A' rather than 'RTA 151G' when completing my pay and display. 

I arrived at the space at 5:55 and, after paying in full for 'CMX 981A', left my car. I returned to find a ticket for not paying. 

As I am not a repeat offender, I believe that my ticket should be cancelled because it was clear that I had paid (albeit misstating the registration) and I was not causing any significant harm to the Milton Keynes community. It is therefore clear that this PCN was issued for a mis-stated registration, making the PCN trivial and inconsistent with other offences for which the fine is issued usually. 

Though I of course apologise, I believe that the council should exercise fairness in cancelling a ticket that, according to the guidance, is perfectly justified to be cancelled. I will of course endeavour to avoid this again, but I feel that the issue of a ticket is a disproportionate action since I made a valid payment and parked in a way that wasn't harmful to the Milton Keynes community. 


Please let me know if you have any further questions. 

Thank you, 

Alfred George Bloggs
